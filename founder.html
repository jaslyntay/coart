Describe your project and its impact ✦ AI Draft
✦ AI Draft — Review & Edit Before Using
Unwoventhread is a community thrift and upcycling market rooted in Tiong Bahru. We create accessible spaces for Singapore's youth to engage with heritage crafts and sustainable fashion, reducing textile waste while building intergenerational connections. Our market events have attracted over 300 participants and diverted an estimated 400kg of clothing from landfill.
How will you use the grant funding? ✦ AI Draft
✦ AI Draft — Review & Edit Before Using
Funding would be allocated across three areas: venue hire and logistics for four market events (40%), artisan stipends to support participating craftspeople (35%), and community outreach and communications to broaden participation in underserved neighbourhoods (25%).
What makes you the right person to lead this? ✦ AI Draft
✦ AI Draft — Review & Edit Before Using
As a final-year Arts & Sustainability student at NUS, I have spent three years studying the intersection of cultural heritage and environmental practice. I founded Unwoventhread after organising community pop-ups in Tiong Bahru and have since built relationships with over 20 local artisans and five NGO partners.
